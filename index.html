About

Academic

I am a third year physics PhD student at Stanford University working on the DM Radio experiment, a resonant dark matter detector searching for axions and hidden photons. As such, my interests primarily lie in beyond-the-standard-model physics but also in quantum metrology and cosmology. I am currently focusing on designing the third generation of DM Radio which will have sensitivity to DFSZ axions. Before becoming an experimentalist, however, I spent two quarters working on the theoretical side, specifically on the phenomenology of dark matter overdensities in astrophysical contexts.

I graduated from UC Berkeley in 2019 with a bachelor's degree in Physics. My research mostly focused on dark matter axion physics as I worked with Prof. Karl van Bibber and was a member of the HAYSTAC collaboration. My experimental contributions revolved around designing, fabricating, and understanding the resonant microwave cavities that are used to search for the axion. I also worked on more independent phenomenological projects.

CURRICULUM VITAE

Personal

Apart from physics, most of my other interests lie in the German and Scandinavian languages and cultures. I particularly enjoy German music from the romantic period, especially works by Richard Wagner, Franz Schubert, Anton Bruckner, and Richard Strauss. I consider Wagner's operas to be the closest works to the Gesamtkunstwerke (complete works of art) -- a term that he himself coined to describe his goals of creating the supreme work of art. Having also taken courses in German, Middle High German, and Danish, I am interested in the literature and epic poems of the middle ages that ultimately influenced the artists of the germanic romantic era.

I also enjoy playing the piano. I had the privilege of studying with Professor Vinia Tsopela for several years who heavily influenced my appreciation for music. Here's an (old) video of me playing two pieces by Béla Bartók.
