About

Academic

I am an incoming physics PhD student at Stanford University where I plan to focus on particle theory. My interests mostly lie particle phenomenology as I hope to expand our understanding of what exists just beyond the standard model.

I graduated from UC Berkeley in 2019 with a bachelor's degree in Physics. My research mostly focused on dark matter axion physics as I worked with Prof. Karl van Bibber and was a member of the HAYSTAC collaboration. My experimental contributions revolved around designing, fabricating, and understanding the resonant microwave cavities that are used to search for the axion. I also worked on more independent phenomenological projects.

CURRICULUM VITAE

Personal

Apart from physics, most of my other interests lie in the German and Scandinavian languages and cultures. I particularly enjoy German music from the romantic period, especially works by Richard Wagner, Franz Schubert, Anton Bruckner, and Richard Strauss. I consider Wagner's operas to be the closest works to the Gesamtkunstwerke (complete works of art) -- a term that he himself coined to describe his goals of creating the supreme work of art. Having also taken a course in Middle High German, I have recently developed an interest in the literature and epic poems produced during that period.

I also enjoy playing the piano. I had the privilege of studying with Professor Vinia Tsopela for several years who heavily influenced my appreciation for music. Here's a video of me playing two pieces by Béla Bartók.
