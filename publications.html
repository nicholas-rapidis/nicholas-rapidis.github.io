Publications

Here is a list of all my publications to date. This list is also available on INSPIRE.

2018

  • Completion of Phase I and Preparation for Phase II of the HAYSTAC Experiment.

    • N.M. Rapidis. Contributed to the 14th Patras Workshop on Axions, WIMPs and WISPs, DESY in Hamburg, June 18 to 22, 2018. arXiv:1809.05913 [physics.ins-det]

    Proceedings from my talk at the 14th Patras workshop where I spoke on behalf of the HAYSTAC collaboration. This work briefly discusses the initial results of HAYSTAC and our upgrades for upcoming runs.

  • Characterization of the HAYSTAC axion dark matter search cavity using microwave measurement and simulation techniques.

    • N.M. Rapidis, et al. Submitted to Review of Scientific Instruments. arXiv:1809.02246 [physics.ins-det]

    A comprehensive review of our understanding of the HAYSTAC cavity. This work represents the first attempt to characterize haloscope cavities using quantitative measurements and simulations.

  • Application of the Bead Perturbation Technique to a Study of a Tuneable 5 GHz Annular Cavity.

    • N.M. Rapidis (2018) In: Carosi G., Rybka G., van Bibber K. (eds) Microwave Cavities and Detectors for Axion Research. Springer Proceedings in Physics, vol 211. Springer, Cham.

    These are my proceedings for the 2nd Workshop on Microwave Cavities and Detectors for Axion Research. They discuss preliminary work on understanding our microwave cavities.

  • Results from phase 1 of the HAYSTAC microwave cavity axion experiment.

    • L. Zhong, et al., Phys. Rev. D. 97, 092001 (2018).

    Our most recent results that, in total, exclude 200 MHz (0.85 μeV) of axion masses at 2.7 x KSVZ.

2017

  • Design and Operational Experience of a Microwave Cavity Axion Detector for the 20-100 μeV Range.

    • S. Al Kenany, et al, Nuclear Instruments and Methods in Physics Research A854, (2017) 11-24.

    A technical paper on our experimental apparatus and procedure during the first run.

  • First Results from a Microwave Cavity Axion Search at 24 μeV.

    • B.M. Brubaker, et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 118, 061302 (2017).

    This is our collaboration's first publication. We are the first experiment to exclude dark matter axions with masses above 10 μeV.
